--- layout: post --- # CoderDojoDC CoderDojoDC is part of the free, open, and non-profit CoderDojo movement to teach kids 9-17 how to code. Best way to learn more is to watch a video: {% raw %} {% endraw %} If you’ve got a kid who wants to learn how to code or if you’d be interested in teaching or just getting involved, join our mailing list and follow us at [@CoderDojoDC](https://twitter.com/CoderDojoDC). …What we need: 1. Parents/Volunteers/Mentors to teach Scratch, HTML, JavaScript, Rasberry Pi, Edubuntu, Python, Makey Makey, Minecraft, Hackety Hack, Alice, Processing, Blockly, Lego Mindstorms …anything the kids want to learn and we can find a volunteer to teach! 2. Old laptops for students who can’t bring their own, preferably with at least 2 GB RAM and 100 GB Hard Drive - we’ll do regular sessions on salvaging old machines with the free Ubuntu Linux Operating System! 3. …oh and kids, 9-17! (12 and under must be accompanied by a parent, but parents of all attendees are strongly encouraged to participate!) …meanwhile, http://CoderDojoNYC.com should give you a pretty good idea of what we hope to do here in the D.C. area.
